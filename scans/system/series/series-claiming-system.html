Series Claiming System

1. Merit Points System

  1. Staff earn points for completing regular chapters while meeting deadlines:
    • Regular chapter: 10 points
    • Speed run chapter: 15 points
    • Booting chapter: 20 points
    • Substitution chapter: 25 points

Example scenario:

Regular chapter completed on time: 10 points
Speed run participation (2 chapters): 30 points
Batch release contribution (5 chapters): 100 points
Substitution chapter completed on time: 25 points
Total: 165 points
  1. Skill-based multipliers apply to point earnings:
    • Novice level: 1.0x (base points)
    • Intermediate level: 1.2x
    • Advanced level: 1.5x
    • Expert level: 1.8x
    • Master level: 2.0x

Example Scenario:

Scenario: Completing a regular chapter (10 base points)

Novice Translator: 10 × 1.0 = 10 points
Intermediate Cleaner: 10 × 1.2 = 12 points
Advanced Editor: 10 × 1.5 = 15 points
Expert Proofreader: 10 × 1.8 = 18 points
Master Project Manager: 10 × 2.0 = 20 points

Skill levels are determined through a combination of experience, consistency of high-quality work, and periodic skill assessments. Staff members can progress through levels over time.

  1. Point decay:
    • All points gradually decrease over time to maintain a dynamic point economy.
    • Active members: 2% of total points decay per week.
    • Inactive members: 10% of total points decay per week.
    • Inactivity is defined as no point-earning actions for 7 consecutive days.
    • Minimum point balance is maintained at 50 points (no decay below this threshold).

Example scenarios:

Scenario 1: Consistent activity
Staff member A starts with 1000 points and remains active:
Week 0: 1000 points
Week 1: 980 points (2% decay: 1000 - 20)
Week 2: 960 points (2% decay: 980 - 20)
Week 3: 941 points (2% decay: 960 - 19)

Scenario 2: Inactivity
Staff member B starts with 1000 points and becomes inactive:
Week 0: 1000 points
Week 1: 900 points (10% decay: 1000 - 100)
Week 2: 810 points (10% decay: 900 - 90)
Week 3: 729 points (10% decay: 810 - 81)

Scenario 3: Mixed activity
Staff member C starts with 1000 points:
Week 0: 1000 points
Week 1: 980 points (active, 2% decay: 1000 - 20)
Week 2: 882 points (inactive, 10% decay: 980 - 98)
Week 3: 864 points (active again, 2% decay: 882 - 18)

Scenario 4: Minimum balance protection
Staff member E has 120 points and is inactive:
Week 0: 120 points
Week 1: 108 points (10% decay: 120 - 12)
Week 2: 97 points (10% decay: 108 - 11)
Week 3: 87 points (10% decay: 97 - 10)
Week 4: 78 points (10% decay: 87 - 9)
Week 5: 70 points (10% decay: 78 - 8)
Week 6: 63 points (10% decay: 70 - 7)
Week 7: 57 points (10% decay: 63 - 6)
Week 8: 51 points (10% decay: 57 - 6)
Week 9: 50 points (minimum balance reached, no further decay)

This accelerated decay system encourages both regular participation and point utilization. Staff can slow down their point decay by remaining active and can protect points by using them for series claims. The system ensures a more dynamic point economy while still allowing members to accumulate points for larger bids.

2. Series Claiming Process

  1. Auction-style bidding using merit points
    • Auctions are held after booting for new series.
    • Auctions are held when needed for unclaimed series.
    • Each auction lasts for 48 hours.
    • Minimum bid is 100 points, may be higher for popular titles.
    • Bids are placed in increments of 50 points.
    • Highest bidder at the end of the auction claims the series.
    • In case of a tie, the earlier bid wins.
    • 10% of the winning bid is deducted as a "transaction fee" to maintain point economy.

Example auction process:

Series: "Caffiene"
Minimum bid: 200 points
Duration: 48 hours

Bidding history:
Hour 01: Staff A bids 200 points
Hour 05: Staff B bids 250 points
Hour 12: Staff C bids 350 points
Hour 24: Staff A bids 400 points
Hour 36: Staff B bids 450 points
Hour 47: Staff C bids 500 points

Result: 
Winner: Staff C with 500 points
Points deducted from Staff C: 550 points (500 bid + 50 transaction fee)
Staff A and B have their bids fully refunded

Staff can set up auto-bidding with a maximum limit to avoid constantly monitoring the auction.

  1. Tiered bidding system:
    • Series are categorized into five tiers based on popularity, complexity, and workload
    • Higher tier series require larger minimum bids and have higher point rewards per chapter

Tier system breakdown:

Tier Category Minimum Bid
S Top popularity, highest complexity 1000 points
A High popularity, very complex 750 points
B Moderate popularity, complex 500 points
C Average popularity, standard complexity 250 points
D Low popularity, simpler works 100 points

Factors determining tier placement:

  • Popularity: Based on reader statistics, social media engagement, and community recognition.
  • Complexity: refer to Series Levels.
  • Tier assignments are reviewed quarterly and may be adjusted based on changes in popularity or complexity.
  • Staff can suggest tier reassessments with supporting evidence.
  • New series are initially placed in tier C unless they have significant pre-existing popularity or complexity.
  1. Claiming window for other staff series opens every three months.
  1. Safe series allocation:
    • Based on staff skill level and recent performance
    • Managers have a higher number of safe series
    • Safe series are protected from auctions and point decay
    • Allocation reviewed quarterly by admin team
    • Maximum of 4 safe series for regular staff, 5 for masters and managers exceptions.
    • Staff must maintain minimum activity levels to keep safe series status

Example safe series distribution:

Novice staff: 0-1 safe series
Intermediate staff: 1-2 safe series
Advanced staff: 2-3 safe series
Expert staff: 3-4 safe series
Master staff: 4-5 safe series
  1. Series exchange market:
    • Between auctions, staff can trade series with or without point exchanges.
    • Trades must be approved by a manager to ensure fairness and maintain quality.
    • Point exchanges in trades are subject to a 5% transaction fee.
    • Staff can list series they're willing to trade in the internal marketplace.
    • Safe series cannot be traded unless a staff member voluntarily gives up the safe status.
    • A cooldown period of 30 days applies before newly acquired series can be traded again.

Example trades:

1. Direct swap: 
   Staff A trades "Manhwa X" for Staff B's "Manhwa Y" (no points involved)

2. Points + Series trade:
   Staff C trades "Manhwa Z" + 300 points to Staff D for "Popular Manhwa W"
   (Staff C pays 315 points due to 5% transaction fee)

This will allow staff to better match the series they like and cooldown tension between them.

3. Implementation

  1. Transparent point tracking system using the points bot:

    • Real-time updates of point balances and series ownership.

  2. Feedback mechanism:

    • Regular staff surveys to gather input on system improvements

  3. Regular system reviews:

    • Regular assessment and adjustment of point values and rules

  4. Dispute resolution process:

    • Staff should try solving their dispute first, if it escalates, the manager will get involved, if one the two parties disagree, and admin will review the whole situation and force their judgment.

This system is designed to be dynamic and adaptable. Regular reviews and adjustments based on team feedback and series needs ensure it continues to motivate staff and improve overall scanlation quality and efficiency.
